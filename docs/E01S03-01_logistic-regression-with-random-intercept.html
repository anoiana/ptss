‚úë Logistic Regression With Random Intercept

üìÖ 2021-07-10

DRAFT

Let us deem logistic model as follows \[ \Bbb{P}(y_{ij}=1|b_i) = \mu(\lambda) = \mu(u+{\boldsymbol{\beta}}^{\top}\boldsymbol{x}_{ij}) = \frac{\exp\{u+{\boldsymbol{\beta}}^{\top}\boldsymbol{x}_{ij}\}}{1+\exp\{u+{\boldsymbol{\beta}}^{\top}\boldsymbol{x}_{ij}\}}, \] we can simplify \(\mu^y(\lambda)[1-\mu(\lambda)]^{1-y}\) to \[ \exp\{y\lambda\}(1-\exp\{\lambda\})^{-1}. \]

We now consider the following likelihood function \[ \begin{align} L(\boldsymbol{\beta}, \sigma^2) &= \prod_{i=1}^N\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\Big[\prod_{j=1}^{n_i} \exp\{\lambda y_{ij}\}(1-\exp\{\lambda\})^{-1}\Big]\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\sigma^2}}\exp\{-u^2/2\sigma^2\}du \\ &= (2\pi\sigma^2)^{-N/2}\prod_{i=1}^N\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\exp\Big\{\sum_{j}^{n_i}[({\boldsymbol{\beta }}^{\top}\boldsymbol{x}_{ij}+u) y_{ij}-\ln(1-e^{({\boldsymbol{\beta }}^{\top}\boldsymbol{x}_{ij}+u)})] -u^2/2\sigma^2 \Big\}du \end{align} \] where \(\lambda = {\boldsymbol{\beta }}^{\top}\boldsymbol{x}_{ij}+u\). Taking natural log gives \[ l(\boldsymbol{\beta}, \sigma^2) = -\frac{N}{2}2\pi\sigma^2 + {\boldsymbol{\beta}}^{\top}\boldsymbol{r} + \sum_{i=1}^N\ln\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}e^{h_i(\boldsymbol{\beta},u)}du \] where \[ h_i(\boldsymbol{\beta},u) = k_iu - \frac{u^2}{2\sigma^2}-\sum_{j=1}^{n_i}\ln(1+e^{{\boldsymbol{\beta}}^{\top}\boldsymbol{x}_{ij}+u}). \] where \(k_i = \sum_{j=1}^{n_i}y_{ij}\) and \(\boldsymbol{r} = \sum_{i,j}y_{ij}\boldsymbol{x}_{ij}\), note that first scalar of \(\boldsymbol{x}_{ij}\) is 1. Also, we realize that \(\{k_i\}\) and \(\boldsymbol{r}\) are jointly sufficient statistic of \((\boldsymbol{\beta},u)\) (where \(u\) is \(\beta_0\)). To maximize \(l(\boldsymbol{\beta},\sigma^2)\), we need to take derivative of the function w.r.t \(\boldsymbol{\beta}\) and \(\sigma^2\), \[ \frac{\partial l}{\partial \boldsymbol{\beta}} = \boldsymbol{r} - \sum_{i=1}^N \frac{\boldsymbol{I}_{i3}}{I_{i1}},\quad \frac{\partial l}{\partial l^2} = -\frac{N}{2\sigma^2}+\frac{1}{2\sigma^2}\sum_{i=1}^N\frac{I_{i2}}{I_{i1}} \] where \[ I_{i1} = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\exp\{h_i(\boldsymbol{\beta};u)\}du, \quad I_{i2} = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}u^2\exp\{h_i(\boldsymbol{\beta};u)\}du, \\ \boldsymbol{I}_{i3} = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\bigg[\sum_{j=1}^{n_i}\boldsymbol{x}_{ij}\frac{\exp({\boldsymbol{\beta}}^{\top}\boldsymbol{x}_{ij}+u)}{1+\exp({\boldsymbol{\beta}}^{\top}\boldsymbol{x}_{ij}+u)}e^{h_i({\boldsymbol{\beta}}^{\top};u)}\bigg]du. \]

The Fisher information for \(\boldsymbol{\beta}\) is approximated as \[ -\Bbb{E}\Big(\frac{\partial^2 l}{\partial\boldsymbol{\beta}^2}\Big) \approx \boldsymbol{H} = \sum_{i=1}^N \Big[\frac{1}{I_{i1}}\boldsymbol{I}_{i4}-\frac{1}{I^2_{i1}}\boldsymbol{I}_{i3}{\boldsymbol{I}}^{\top}_{i3} \Big] \] where \[ \boldsymbol{I}_{i4} = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\Big[\sum_{j=1}^{n_i}\boldsymbol{x}_{ij}{\boldsymbol{x}}^{\top}_{ij}\frac{\exp({\boldsymbol{\beta}}^{\top}\boldsymbol{x}_{ij}+u)}{(1+\exp({\boldsymbol{\beta}}^{\top}\boldsymbol{x}_{ij}+u))^2}e^{h_i(\boldsymbol{\beta},u)}\Big]du \]
